# Purpose

This document defines the long-term vision for Spot & Report.

It explains why the product exists, the problem it aims to solve and the principles that guide its evolution.

Unlike the Engineering Constitution, which defines how the project is built, this document defines why the product deserves to exist.

Every product decision should support the vision described here.

As the product matures, this document may evolve to reflect new learning while remaining true to its core purpose.

---

# Vision

Spot & Report exists to make reporting important real-world incidents dramatically simpler.

People should be able to recognise an incident, capture the relevant information and prepare a high-quality report in under sixty seconds.

The experience should feel approachable, trustworthy and effortless.

The technology behind the platform may be sophisticated, but the experience should remain intentionally simple.

Over time, Spot & Report aims to become a flexible incident reporting platform capable of supporting governments, councils, organisations and communities around the world.

Every new workflow should maintain the same commitment to simplicity, accessibility and trust established by the first.

---

# Mission

Our mission is to remove unnecessary friction from public incident reporting.

When members of the public are willing to report something important, the reporting process should help them—not discourage them.

Spot & Report combines intuitive design with responsible artificial intelligence to help people prepare clear, structured and useful reports without requiring technical knowledge or familiarity with reporting systems.

The platform is designed to improve the quality of information reaching organisations while reducing the effort required from the people submitting it.

---

# The Problem

Communities depend on people reporting what they observe.

Whether it is a sick bird, illegal dumping, flooding, damaged infrastructure or another environmental issue, early reporting allows organisations to respond more effectively.

Despite this, many reporting systems remain unnecessarily difficult to use.

People are often required to:

- Determine which organisation is responsible.
- Navigate unfamiliar websites.
- Complete lengthy forms.
- Enter repetitive information.
- Describe incidents without guidance.
- Attach supporting evidence manually.
- Understand technical terminology.

These barriers reduce reporting rates and often result in incomplete or inconsistent information.

Many people simply decide that reporting the incident is not worth the effort.

The result is not a lack of community willingness.

It is unnecessary friction.

---

# Our Belief

We believe reporting important incidents should be as simple as taking a photo and answering a few clear questions.

People should not need to understand government structures, reporting processes or technical terminology to help protect their communities.

Good software removes complexity rather than exposing it.

Artificial intelligence should quietly improve the quality of reports without making the experience feel more complicated.

The success of Spot & Report will not be measured by how advanced the technology becomes.

It will be measured by how little users need to think about the technology at all.

---

# Why Now

Several important trends have converged.

Modern smartphones now provide high-quality cameras, accurate location services and fast internet connectivity almost everywhere.

Artificial intelligence has matured to the point where it can assist people in organising information, improving clarity and preparing structured reports within seconds.

At the same time, environmental challenges, biosecurity risks and community infrastructure issues increasingly depend on timely reporting from members of the public.

Technology now has the opportunity to reduce the effort required to participate in protecting our communities.

Rather than replacing existing organisations, Spot & Report aims to strengthen them by helping people submit better information more consistently.

---

# Our Approach

Spot & Report does not attempt to replace existing reporting systems.

Instead, it improves what happens before a report reaches the responsible organisation.

The platform guides users through a simple, structured reporting process by combining:

- Photos
- Location
- Guided questions
- Context-aware prompts
- Responsible AI assistance

The result is a report that is more complete, more consistent and easier for organisations to assess.

The platform focuses on improving report quality while keeping the reporting experience approachable for everyone.

Technology should quietly support the user rather than becoming the focus of the experience.

---

# Why Spot & Report?

The name reflects the behaviour rather than the technology.

People do not need to understand how the platform works.

They simply need to understand what they need to do.

Spot it.

Report it.

The entire product experience is built around those two actions.

Like Hide & Seek, the name describes a familiar human behaviour instead of a technical solution.

The product should feel immediately understandable regardless of age, technical ability or previous experience.

Its purpose is not to impress users with technology.

# The First Workflow

The first implementation of Spot & Report focuses on reporting sick or dead wild birds.

This workflow has been chosen because it addresses a genuine public need while providing a practical demonstration of the wider platform.

The workflow is intentionally focused.

A user should be able to:

1. Open Spot & Report.
2. Select the Bird Report workflow.
3. Take or upload a photo.
4. Confirm the incident location.
5. Answer a small number of guided questions.
6. Review an AI-assisted report.
7. Submit the completed report to the appropriate organisation.

Completing this workflow in under sixty seconds is a primary design objective.

The Bird Report workflow is not the long-term product.

It is the first demonstration of a platform designed to support many different reporting scenarios.

---

# Long-Term Platform Vision

The technology developed for the Bird Report workflow is intentionally designed to support additional incident types without changing the overall user experience.

Future workflows may include:

- Wildlife incidents
- Biosecurity threats
- Invasive species
- Illegal dumping
- Flooding
- Storm damage
- Environmental hazards
- Road hazards
- Community infrastructure issues
- Public safety observations

Each workflow should follow the same fundamental experience:

- Observe an incident.
- Capture evidence.
- Answer guided questions.
- Review the generated report.
- Submit with confidence.

Regardless of the incident type, the reporting experience should remain familiar and predictable.

The long-term objective is not to create many different applications.

It is to create one trusted platform capable of supporting many reporting workflows.

Spot & Report is designed for people who want to help their communities.

This includes:

- Members of the public.
- Volunteers.
- Conservation groups.
- Community organisations.
- Local councils.
- Government agencies.
- Environmental organisations.

# Success Measures

The success of Spot & Report should be measured by outcomes rather than feature count.

Indicators of success include:

- A first-time user understands what to do immediately.
- A complete report can be prepared in under sixty seconds.
- Reports contain higher-quality information than traditional manual submissions.
- Users feel confident submitting reports.
- Organisations receive clearer, more consistent information.
- The platform reduces unnecessary reporting friction.
- Additional workflows can be introduced without increasing product complexity.

Long-term success will be measured by the positive impact the platform creates for communities rather than the number of features it contains.

# Guiding Principles

Every evolution of Spot & Report should remain consistent with the following principles.

## Simplicity First

The simplest successful experience is usually the best experience.

Reduce effort before adding capability.

---

## Solve Real Problems

Build features that solve genuine reporting challenges experienced by real users.

Avoid features that exist only because they are technically interesting.

---

## Trust Through Transparency

Represent the platform honestly.

Clearly communicate what AI can and cannot do.

Never imply certainty where uncertainty exists.

---

## Human-Centred Design

Technology should support people rather than demand their attention.

The reporting experience should feel calm, intuitive and approachable.

---

## Accessible by Default

Accessibility should be considered throughout design and implementation.

An accessible reporting experience benefits everyone.

---

## Responsible Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence should improve clarity, consistency and report quality.

It should never replace human judgement or create information that cannot be supported.

---

## Build for Longevity

Design today's workflow carefully so tomorrow's workflows can build upon proven foundations.

Expand only after existing experiences have been validated.

# Final Statement

Spot & Report is founded on a simple belief:

When people are willing to help their communities, technology should make that contribution easier—not harder.

The platform exists to remove unnecessary barriers between observing an incident and reporting it responsibly.

Every workflow should be designed with the same commitment to simplicity, accessibility and trust.

Every new capability should strengthen that commitment rather than dilute it.
