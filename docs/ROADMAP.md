# Spot & Report Roadmap

> The planned evolution of Spot & Report from a focused reporting MVP into a flexible incident reporting platform.

---

**Document:** ROADMAP.md

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Living

**Owner:** Project Owner

**Last Updated:** 19 July 2026

---

# Purpose

This roadmap describes the intended evolution of Spot & Report.

It communicates the long-term direction of the platform while keeping the immediate focus on delivering a polished, production-quality MVP.

The roadmap is intended to guide future development rather than prescribe fixed delivery dates.

Real-world feedback should always take precedence over speculative planning.

---

# Roadmap Philosophy

Roadmaps describe intent—not promises.

The sequence of work may change as new information becomes available.

Product principles defined in `docs/PRINCIPLES.md` always take precedence over roadmap items.

The objective is not to deliver the greatest number of features.

The objective is to deliver the greatest amount of value with the least unnecessary complexity.

---

# Evolution Strategy

Spot & Report will evolve through a series of capability stages.

Each stage builds upon the previous one while preserving the core product principles:

- Simplicity.
- Trust.
- Reliability.
- Responsible AI.
- Mobile-first design.

The platform should grow through validated learning rather than speculative engineering.

# Stage 1 — Prove the Experience

**Objective**

Demonstrate that reporting real-world incidents can be dramatically simpler through a focused, AI-assisted workflow.

**Primary Outcome**

A polished Build Week MVP capable of guiding users from observation to report submission in under 60 seconds.

**Key Capabilities**

- Bird Report workflow.
- Mobile-first reporting experience.
- Image capture and upload.
- Location confirmation.
- Guided questions.
- AI-assisted report generation.
- User review.
- Report submission.
- Confirmation experience.

**Success Criteria**

- End-to-end reporting demonstrated successfully.
- Reliable AI-assisted report generation.
- Clear user experience.
- Production-quality implementation.
- Strong technical documentation.

---

# Stage 2 — Validate Real-World Use

**Objective**

Learn from real users and improve reporting quality through practical deployment.

**Primary Outcome**

Validate that the workflow provides measurable value outside a demonstration environment.

**Potential Enhancements**

- Improved report routing.
- Better submission reliability.
- User feedback collection.
- Analytics.
- AI prompt refinement.
- Report quality improvements.
- Performance optimisation.
- Accessibility improvements.

**Success Criteria**

- Positive user feedback.
- Improved reporting completion rates.
- Stable production operation.
- Evidence of real-world usefulness.

---

# Stage 3 — Expand Incident Types

**Objective**

Extend the reporting platform beyond the initial bird-report workflow.

**Potential Workflows**

- Sick wildlife.
- Dead wildlife.
- Illegal dumping.
- Flooding.
- Storm damage.
- Road hazards.
- Environmental hazards.
- Biosecurity incidents.
- Community infrastructure issues.

Each workflow should reuse the existing reporting architecture while remaining tailored to its specific reporting requirements.

**Success Criteria**

- Multiple high-quality workflows.
- Consistent reporting experience.
- Shared platform components.
- Minimal duplicated implementation.

---

# Stage 4 — Connected Reporting

**Objective**

Improve how reports reach the organisations responsible for responding.

**Potential Capabilities**

- Multiple submission destinations.
- Organisation-specific adapters.
- Government integrations.
- API-based submissions.
- Intelligent routing.
- Report tracking.
- Notifications.
- Status updates.

The platform should continue preparing reports rather than becoming a replacement for existing organisational systems.

**Success Criteria**

- Reliable routing.
- Reduced manual handling.
- Improved report quality.
- Better communication with receiving organisations.

---

# Stage 5 — Intelligent Incident Platform

**Objective**

Transform Spot & Report into a reusable platform capable of supporting many reporting domains.

**Potential Capabilities**

- Multilingual reporting.
- Organisation portals.
- Administrative dashboards.
- Workflow configuration.
- Advanced AI assistance.
- Operational analytics.
- Open APIs.
- Third-party integrations.

The focus remains on reducing reporting friction rather than increasing platform complexity.

**Success Criteria**

- Flexible platform architecture.
- Broad workflow support.
- Responsible AI at scale.
- High-quality reporting across multiple domains.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The long-term ambition is not to become another reporting system.

The ambition is to become the simplest way for people to report important real-world incidents regardless of incident type or receiving organisation.

As the platform grows, users should continue to experience the same qualities that define the initial MVP:

- Fast.
- Simple.
- Trustworthy.
- Guided.
- AI-assisted.
- Human-centred.

Growth should increase capability—not complexity.

---

# Guiding Priorities

When deciding what to build next, priority should generally be given to work that:

1. Improves the reporting experience.
2. Reduces user effort.
3. Strengthens trust.
4. Improves report quality.
5. Simplifies maintenance.
6. Enables future workflows through reusable architecture.

Features that do not contribute meaningfully to these priorities should be reconsidered.

---

# Measuring Progress

Progress should be evaluated through outcomes rather than feature counts.

Representative measures include:

- Reporting completion rate.
- Average reporting time.
- Report quality.
- AI assistance reliability.
- User satisfaction.
- Successful submissions.
- Workflow reuse.
- Platform maintainability.

Building more functionality does not necessarily represent meaningful progress.

---

# What Is Not on the Roadmap

The roadmap intentionally avoids committing to:

- fixed release dates,
- speculative technologies,
- unnecessary infrastructure,
- platform features without demonstrated need,
- or capabilities that conflict with the principles defined in `docs/PRINCIPLES.md`.

The roadmap should remain adaptable as the product evolves.

---

# Final Principle

A roadmap should inspire direction without restricting learning.

Spot & Report will continue evolving as new reporting challenges emerge.

The destination is not defined by the number of features delivered.

It is defined by how effectively the platform helps people report important incidents with confidence.

When faced with competing priorities, build the feature that makes reporting simpler.
